Melanie Perkins

CEO of Canva

Melanie Perkins is an Australian technology entrepreneur and the co-founder and CEO of Canva, a graphic design platform that has revolutionized the way people create visual content.

Born in Perth, Australia, Melanie Perkins began her entrepreneurial journey at the age of 19. She co-founded Canva in 2012 with the vision of democratizing design and making it accessible to everyone. Under her leadership, Canva has grown into a multi-billion dollar company used by millions worldwide.

Perkins is known for her innovative approach to business and her commitment to creating a positive company culture. Her success story serves as an inspiration to aspiring entrepreneurs, especially in the tech industry.

Key Achievements

  • Co-founded Canva, valued at over $40 billion as of 2021
  • Named one of Forbes' "Top Under 30 of the Decade" in 2020
  • Recognized as one of Fortune's "40 Under 40" in 2020
  • Led Canva to over 60 million monthly active users across 190 countries

Dishanta Kpatrick

Full Stack Software Engineer at Intuit Mailchimp

Dishanta Kpatrick's journey into tech is a testament to the power of self-learning, resilience, and community support.

From teaching herself to code while working various jobs to completing a full-stack bootcamp and eventually landing a role as a Full Stack Engineer at Intuit Mailchimp, Dishanta’s story highlights the importance of persistence in overcoming barriers.

Her experience underscores the value of non-traditional paths, apprenticeship opportunities, and mentorship in tech, offering a powerful message to aspiring engineers: with passion and determination, success in tech is attainable, no matter where you start.

Emmanuela Ilok

Founder of CodED

Emmanuela Ilok from Nigeria has earned full scholarships to several prestigious universities, including MIT, Stanford, Yale, Princeton, UPenn, and Columbia, due to her exceptional academic and technological achievements.

Her journey began with top performance in the International General Certificate of Secondary Education (IGCSE) in Nigeria, where she was recognized by the British Council. Ilok went on to develop software using Machine Learning to detect breast cancer with 91% accuracy, showcasing her talent in tech innovation. At just 16, she won the prestigious RISE program with her project “CodEd,” which aimed to improve computer science education in Nigerian public schools through teacher training and student internships. Her accomplishments have been praised by education advocate Alex Onyia, and she now serves as an inspiration for Nigerian students, sharing her experience and offering guidance on pursuing global educational opportunities.

Fei-Fei Li

Sequoia Capital Professor

Dr. Fei-Fei Li is a Stanford Professor of Computer Science and Co-Director of the Human-Centered AI Institute.

Known for her pioneering work in computer vision and AI, she co-created ImageNet and served as Chief Scientist at Google Cloud. She is also a strong advocate for diversity in STEM, co-founding AI4ALL to promote inclusive AI education.

Dishanta Kpatrick

Dishanta Kpatrick is a Full Stack Software Engineer at Intuit Mailchimp, known for her resilience as a Black woman navigating the tech industry. She faced numerous obstacles on her path, including financial challenges that initially limited her educational choices. Without access to student loans, she pursued self-taught coding, repeatedly stopping and starting her education before finally securing a position at Intuit. There, she progressed from tech support to engineering, taking advantage of apprenticeship opportunities to solidify her skills.

In her role, Dishanta not only contends with the technical demands of engineering but also advocates for representation. She has found community support through Intuit’s Employee Resource Groups, where she connects with other Black professionals in tech. Her experiences reflect a broader journey for Black women in tech, facing both gender and racial barriers, yet she has persevered, underscoring the importance of representation and support networks in overcoming these challenges​.

Emmanuela Ilok

Emmanuela Ilok, a Nigerian teenager, has made significant strides in technology and education, tackling issues that resonate with her experiences as a young Black woman in tech. Awarded full scholarships to prestigious institutions like MIT and Stanford, she is dedicated to reforming computer science education in Nigeria, where she noticed disparities between public and private schools. Her Rise project, “CodEd,” is a program that offers teacher training, student-centered curricula, and internship opportunities aimed at making tech education more accessible and practical in underfunded schools.

Ilok has also developed tech solutions addressing local challenges, such as an app prototype to enhance safety amid Nigeria’s insecurity concerns. Her journey highlights challenges often faced by young women of color in STEM fields, particularly around age-based discrimination and limited resources. Despite these obstacles, she has continued to advocate for equitable education and inspire other Nigerian students to pursue their passions in technology, showing how tenacity and a commitment to social impact can drive meaningful change in underrepresented communities​

Fei-Fei Li

Fei-Fei Li is an Asian woman in a field historically dominated by white men. Her journey through AI reflects the challenges faced by someone who is both a woman and a person of color in tech. The barriers she has overcome to become a leading expert in artificial intelligence exemplify how gender and race intersect to create unique challenges.

Li has spoken out about the lack of diversity in AI development, highlighting how bias in datasets can lead to AI systems that disadvantage marginalized groups. Through her work with **AI4ALL**, she directly addresses these issues by fostering more diversity and representation in AI, focusing on young women and students of color. AI4ALL’s mission is to empower underrepresented groups by providing access to AI education.

Li’s initiatives target the intersectional barriers faced by underrepresented groups in tech, working to ensure that people of diverse backgrounds—who are often overlooked—can contribute to shaping the future of AI. Her efforts exemplify how technology can address the compounded disadvantages of race, gender, and class when thoughtfully applied.
